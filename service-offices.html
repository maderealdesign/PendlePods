Got Questions?

Garden Office FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about building a dedicated workspace at home.

How do I get internet access in my garden office?

During the groundwork phase, we lay an armored data cable (CAT6) alongside your electrical supply in the trench. This hardwires your garden office directly to your home router, ensuring you get maximum speeds with zero Wi-Fi dropouts—perfect for seamless Zoom calls and large file transfers.

Will the garden office be warm enough to work in during winter?

Yes. We utilize thick PIR rigid insulation in the walls, floor, and roof, creating an incredibly thermally efficient environment. Paired with high-performance double glazing and an optional smart panel heater or air-conditioning unit, your office will be warm and comfortable even on the coldest winter mornings.

Do I need planning permission for a garden office?

The vast majority of our garden offices fall under 'Permitted Development' and do not require planning permission. Our standard designs keep the overall height under 2.5 meters to comply with these regulations. We will comprehensively assess your garden and confirm this for your specific property during our free site survey.

Can I claim a garden office as a business expense?

If you are a limited company director or self-employed, you may be able to claim certain costs against your business, including VAT reclamation on the build and ongoing running costs. However, tax rules are complex and depend heavily on whether the structure has personal use alongside business use. We always recommend consulting a qualified accountant to understand your specific tax benefits.
