Got Questions?

Workshop Build FAQs

Technical answers for serious makers planning their new garden workspace.

Can the workshop floor support heavy machinery like a lathe or table saw?

Absolutely. For workshop builds, we specify heavy-duty floor joists set at tighter centers and utilize thicker structural OSB or plywood decking. This ensures a rigid, bounce-free foundation capable of supporting heavy woodworking machinery, metalworking tools, and workbenches without compromising the structure over time.

Can you provide a high-capacity power supply for power tools?

Yes. We run a dedicated, high-capacity armored cable from your house consumer unit directly to a new sub-board inside the workshop. We design the circuit layout around your specific needs, providing ample 13A double sockets, and can even provision for dedicated 16A commando sockets or 3-phase power setups if your machinery demands it.

Are garden workshops secure enough for expensive tools?

Security is a top priority for our workshop builds. Unlike flimsy shed doors with basic padlocks, we install premium uPVC or Aluminum doors with high-security multi-point locking mechanisms. We also suggest minimizing window size or using high-level "letterbox" windows to keep prying eyes out, whilst maintaining essential natural light.

Do I need planning permission to build a garden workshop?

In most domestic settings, a garden workshop falls under 'Permitted Development' and does not require planning permission, provided it is under 2.5m tall and intended for hobby or personal use. However, if you are running a full-time commercial manufacturing business from it, or have clients visiting, regulations may differ. We will advise you fully during your free site survey.
