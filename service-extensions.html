Extension FAQs

Common Questions

Planning an extension is a major decision. Here is everything you need to know about our timber frame build process.

Why choose a timber frame extension over traditional brick?

Timber frame extensions offer significantly faster build times on-site, superior thermal insulation for lower heating bills, and a lower carbon footprint. Despite the timber structure, they can be finished externally in brick slips, render, or timber cladding to perfectly match or contrast your existing home.

Do I need planning permission for a home extension?

Many single-story rear extensions fall under 'Permitted Development' rights and do not require full planning permission, provided they meet specific size and boundary criteria. However, larger extensions, side-returns, or homes in conservation areas will require approval. As part of our turnkey service, we guide you through the entire planning and building control process.

How long does a timber frame extension take to build?

Because the core timber frame is manufactured and pre-cut off-site, the on-site construction phase is remarkably fast. Once foundations are laid, the main structure can often be made watertight within a matter of days. A complete extension, including electrical wiring, plastering, and internal fit-out, typically takes 6-10 weeks depending on size and complexity.

Will the extension look like a garden pod attached to my house?

Not at all unless that is the specific architectural style you desire. We use the exact same structural timber frame engineering principles as our luxury pods, but the exterior finish is completely customizable. We can use matching brickwork, crisp silicone render, or contemporary cedar cladding to achieve the exact aesthetic required to blend with or elevate your property.
